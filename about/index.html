About.

When I was 14 or 15, one of my closest friends pointed me to the original book of “The Anarchist Cookbook” during a conversation we were having about one of our classmate who seemingly got in trouble because of it. Hearing his story unfold, I couldn’t grasp the idea why people were so afraid of it or why they labeled it as “forbidden” or “the-police-will-knock-on-your-door” type of book. I always thought (and still do) that knowledge should be free and accessible to everyone […]

Nevertheless, the scaremongering (obviously) didn’t help at all. In fact, it peeked my interest even more; the concept of “hidden” knowledge strengthened my resolve to go on a quest and find myself a copy of this particular book.

So, I went about looking for this book online. One source led to another and just like that I disovered a different world. A space of bits and bytes which I have never seen before. It’s caught me in its claws ever since. That’s how it all began for me.

As Eric Weinstein would ask: “Where is the Portal?”

An intriguing question, if you think about it […] Just like anyone out there, I was also someone who, at some point, got trapped in a humdrum existence, in what seemed to be an ordinary world. And the story above became my call to adventure through the gateway of my newly discovered lens. I call it the “core” portal because it has become a huge part of my being, and I would encourage you to find yours, if you haven’t yet.

It seems to be the same formula, pattern or format but always in a different context; that this concept or mythology of the “looking glass” or the “rabbit hole” in the Matrix are metaphors for very real things.

So, “What’s this blog about?” - I hear you say? Glad you asked! As Adam Chester mentions on his blog: “It’s a home to all of my personal posts, created throughout my time in InfoSec as part of my belief in open knowledge sharing.” And on top of that, I would also add that it’s meant to be an outlet or a drawing board where I mainly teach myself about new ideas or concepts to further improve my skillsets on (malware) reverse engineering, exploit development, programming and Tactics, Techniques and Procedures (TTPs).

Hope you enjoy the ride.
